Research & Opportunity

A small group of us visited consumers, stores, and subject experts around the world to learn more. We spent time with consumer at their homes in Osaka, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, San Juan PR, Orlanda, Boston, and Dallas to put ourselves in their shoes.

Here's what we found:
 Mostly harmless- Out of 3,500 species of mosquitoes, only 4 like to bite people: aedes aegypti, aedes albopictus, anopheles, and culex fatiqans.

 Girls Bite- Only females mosquitoes bite people (once they’re pregnant). This means that only female mosquitoes transmit contagious diseases. Males mosquitoes feed mostly on sugar water.

 3000 Eggs- Female mosquitoes lay 100-300 eggs at once, up to 10 times which means 1000-3000 offsprings. Female mosquitoes only feed on blood before laying eggs as they need the protein.

 Attractants- Mosquitoes need different things to locate food at long, mid, and short range. CO2 attracts them from 25’+ away. Mid-range attractants like heat works better as the they gets closer. The scent of human sweat helps the mosquito pin point its target.

 One Big Market- China accounts for more than 50% of insect product sales every year. Countries like U.S. and Japan also see high sales volume. A small bug problem seems bigger to consumers who rarely see them.

 Fly by Light- Many insects navigate using the moon light. As a side effect, many man-made, circular lights disorient or even attract flying insects.
