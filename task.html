Workflow Studio

Taskifying mortgage work by turning a complicated and ambiguous process into an information assembly line.

The majority of work time in mortgage is spent on activities related to finding, understanding, and priortizing information.

Research & Opportunity

A basic home purchase loan process looks something like this



It's a complicated process with lots of edge cases and pain points...
The PM, EM, and myself shadowed dozens of mortgage professionals in every stage of the loan process to understand their work flow, concerns, interactions, process and pain points.

Here are some patterns we noticed:
 It depents - The right thing to work on depends on lots of changing variable (capital market conditions, last response, changing borrower intent, etc.)

 Scattered - Information needed to complete mortgage related work is generally scattered across different forms & products - requiring lots of manual digging & confirming

 Expert required- Mortgage work generally require specialized(expensive) workers because the information needed to do work is ambiguous and hard to find.

 Long process- Every loan requires multiple rounds careful combing through borrower information to ensure the information is accurate and threshold are met.
If mortgage work can be broken down to small, contained tasks and be shown with context - then expensive work that used to require specialized professionals can be done by a non-specialized person at greater efficiency with less errors.

Exploring solutions

We kicked off the project with cross functions leads, created, consolidated, & reconciled ideas guided by user testing with prototypes.

After testing successfully on a general design direction, we started working out the different states & flows to address all of the likely scenarios in our MVP use case.

Results

Workflow Builder lets mortgage managers create logic based work flows and assign them to the apprepriate team once loan files meet a specific set of conditions.
Specific tasks are assigned to the appropriate employee on a timeline and cadence determined by the manager.


The resulting task will queue in a list as they match the defined trigger unless managers set priorities for specific traits.

The workflow is then reviewed and published.

Tasklist

Teams can claim, unclaim, and see see claimer of tasks to let multiple members work on the same batch of work if needed. Once a task is done, the next relevant task is automatically triggered and delegated to keep the loan file moving.


Workflow Builder and Tasklist allowed managers to track and continuously optimize their workflow strategies.

Early MVP results showed complex task completion time improve an average of 6.2% (From 57 to 53.5 hours including time in queue).
