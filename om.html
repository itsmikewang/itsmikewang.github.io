Research & Opportunity

We hired scientists and traveled around the world to understand the problem

P&G's nonwoven technology can significantly reduce the cost of air filtration. However, every single research participant noted better sleep and diminished allergy symptoms after using the device for 2 weeks. The sleep category is interesting, big, and growing quickly.

We went through insights from the unlaunched air purifier as well as additional user observations & interviews. After digging into the user struggles and wish-for around the topic of sleep, a few interesting things jumped out:
 Urban problem The struggle to get enough sleep affects urbanites disproportionally. Leading causes are increased work pressure, city noises, & inconsistent routines.

 Morning after Other than interrupted sleep. The quality of a night's sleep is judged based on the feelings of the first few minutes after waking.

 Cool down The average person experience a 2° body temperature drop after falling asleep. The right environment for falling sleep is not as perfect during sleep.

 Consistency consistency A good sleep hygiene - doing the same things at the same time everyday before sleep - is far more effective in creating better sleep than any other controllable factor.

 It's all relative Sleep disturbance results from relative changes in sound, light, & other sensations. Counter-intuitively, this means a quieter, darker, etc., room could makes things worse.

 Addiction concerns Consumers, especially in Asia, refuse to use medication to aid sleep except in desperate situations. Any method where the user can grow increasingly dependent is shunned.
People want good sleep that is natural, consistent, and controllable.
