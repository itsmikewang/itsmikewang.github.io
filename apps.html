Apps Refresh

Driving app re-engagement by exposing relevant and interesting events to Meetuppers

Once a user is in the app, there are lots of common interactions in place to keep them engaged. But what if....

Research & Opportunity

We previously treated empty searches as mistakes, and sent users to a generalized experience where they saw a jobs from a manually curated list of industries. This experience had poor conversion, and we hypothesized that the volume is too big to just be a mistake. So we recruited real job seekers to learn more.

We talked to a bunch of recent job seekers and noticed some common threads:

  Career Fluid- Many entry level workers are not fixated on a specific career yet. A restaurant wait staff is sometimes just as happy to find a retail role next and vice versa.

 Trouble seeing- Many job seekers felt like there must be more job titles they should be exploring but did not know of a easy way to discover them.

 3x drop-off- users who blank search are many times likely to finish their session without applying.

 Feature, not bug- Job boards generally treat empty searches as mistakes, but many job seekers saw them as a tool to explore opportunities.
Job seekers often aren't aware of most of the job titles they should be searching for. Empty searches are often the results of job seekers exploring their options.

Exploring solutions



We ran A/B tests over the course of 3 months to see if nudges and suggestions at key points in the experience would increase application & match rate.

Results

Jumpstarting their search

We tested our way into prompting users with popular and trending search terms to help them discover titles.

Intercepting empty searching

We began to provide job seekers who run an empty search with other parameters to find a job that fits their criterias.

Suggestions Based On User Actions

Upon hover(desktop) and inactivity(mobile) - we started to prompt users to explore similar jobs by presenting links to search pages with similar title, salary, company, and location.

A better way to find jobs

We began prompting empty searchers to sign up for our ML power matching product so they can be fed jobs based on their skills & experience instead of relying on keywords.

3+% lift

Search field suggestion, empty search modal, and related jobs link were all implemented after seeing a 3+% lift in application rate against control in A/B testing. Prompting users to get matched achieved parity but aligned with the broader company KR.
