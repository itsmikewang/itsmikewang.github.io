Jobseeker insights

Turning people into better job seekers with insights based on their peer and hiring counter parts

The more of a job description a job seeker sees, the less likely they are to apply to it.

Research & Opportunity

We tested showing various levels of abbreviating the job description and found some unexpected behaviors:

  The more they read, the less they applied
We A/B tested abbreviating the job description to 5, 3, & 2 lines and found that the less description we showed, the more job seekers tended to apply. Once the user clicked expand to see the full description, it was even worse.

 Job descriptions deter women more
When we broke the data down by user demographic, we realized that women were 2x as likely to not apply once they clicked "read more".

 Copy & pasted descriptions everywhere
In a parallel project, we learned that hiring managers generally copy and paste the majority of their job description from other ones they see. Job seekers might be filtering themselves out because of something the hiring manager barely care about.
Job seekers read descriptions to see if they are a match. So, why not just let them know if they are?

Exploring solutions

We tried a bunch of ideas and relied on crits, usertestings and A/B testing to discover the winning concept.

Results

User centric job cards

We updated our job card designs to expose less of the job description and more of how "you" match by default and planned the roll out in stages.

Profile insights

We expanded on the strategy and added insights to profile to encourage a positive cycle of a more complete profile that generates better insights.
